About the Journal

Polymers are substances containing a large number of structural units joined by the same type of linkage. These substances often form into a chain-like structure. Starch, cellulose, and rubber all possess polymeric properties. Today, the polymer industry has grown to be larger than the aluminum, copper and steel industries combined. Polymers already have a range of applications that far exceeds that of any other class of material available to man.

05 Notes for Contributors
Submission Guidelines+

Each submission must include a signed Copyright Transfer and Disclosure Form, a Title Page, and an Anonymized Manuscript. Articles must not have been submitted elsewhere simultaneously and must not have been previously published in whole or in part.

Manuscript Preparation+

Manuscripts should be typed in Times New Roman 12-point. Required sections in order: Abstract (max 250 words, min 3 keywords), Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion, Acknowledgements, References, Tables, and Figures. All figures must be supplied as separate files at minimum 300 dpi (tiff, jpeg, png, or eps).

Peer-Review Process+

Double-blind peer-review and publication decisions are carried out by the Editors-in-Chief in cooperation with the Editorial Board. Authors receive complimentary PDFs of their final articles upon publication.

Publication Ethics+

SCIENTIA NATURALIS follows all COPE Codes of Conduct. Authors must include an "Ethical Compliance" section before References, declaring funding sources, conflicts of interest, informed consent, human rights compliance, and animal welfare statements as applicable.

Open Access & Optional Services+

There is no charge to submit or publish. Optional paid services include Open Access, color printing, article offprints, discounted print copies, and reuse licenses. These are offered only after acceptance and are never required.
